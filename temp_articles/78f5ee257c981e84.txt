    <title>Alpha-muon sticking and chaos in muon-catalysed "in flight" d-t fusion</title>
    <updated>2006-07-31T11:37:11Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/physics/0605206v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/physics/0605206v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  We discuss the alpha-muon sticking coefficient in the muon-catalysed ``in flight" d-t fusion in the framework of the Constrained Molecular Dynamics model. Especially the influence of muonic chaotic dynamics on the sticking coefficient is brought into focus. The chaotic motion of the muon affects not only the fusion cross section but also the $μ-α$ sticking coefficient. Chaotic systems lead to larger enhancements with respect to regular systems because of the reduction of the tunneling region. Moreover they give smaller sticking probabilities than those of regular events. By utilizing a characteristic of the chaotic dynamics one can avoid losing the muon in the $μ$CF cycle. We propose the application of the so-called ``microwave ionization of a Rydberg atom" to the present case which could lead to the enhancement of the reactivation process by using X-rays.</summary>

    <id>http://arxiv.org/abs/physics/0510034v1</id>
    <title>6D Muon Ionization Cooling with an Inverse Cyclotron</title>
    <updated>2005-10-05T05:46:48Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/physics/0510034v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/physics/0510034v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  A large admittance sector cyclotron filled with LiH wedges surrounded by helium or hydrogen gas is explored. Muons are cooled as they spiral adiabatically into a central swarm. As momentum approaches zero, the momentum spread also approaches zero. Long bunch trains coalesce. Energy loss is used to inject the muons into the outer rim of the cyclotron. The density of material in the cyclotron decreases adiabatically with radius. The sector cyclotron magnetic fields are transformed into an azimuthally symmetric magnetic bottle in the center. Helium gas is used to inhibit muonium formation by positive muons. Deuterium gas is used to allow captured negative muons to escape via the muon catalyzed fusion process. The presence of ionized gas in the center may automatically neutralize space charge. When a bunch train has coalesced into a central swarm, it is ejected axially with an electric kicker pulse.</summary>
