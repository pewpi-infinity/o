    <title>Bandgap Narrowing in Quantum Wires</title>
    <updated>2005-08-11T07:44:21Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/cond-mat/0508279v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/cond-mat/0508279v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  In this paper we consider two different geometry of quasi one-dimensional semiconductors and calculate their exchange-correlation induced bandgap renormalization (BGR) as a function of the electron-hole plasma density and quantum wire width. Based on different fabrication scheme, we define suitable external confinement potential and then leading-order GW dynamical screening approximation is used in the calculation by treating electron-electron Coulomb interaction and electron-optical phonon interaction. Using a numerical scheme, screened Coulomb potential, probability of different states, profile of charge density and the values of the renormalized gap energy are calculated and the effects of variation of confinement potential width and temperature are studied.</summary>

    <title>Highly efficient single photon emission from single quantum dots within a two-dimensional photonic bandgap</title>
    <updated>2007-08-07T12:49:15Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/0708.0944v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/0708.0944v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  We report highly efficient single photon generation from InGaAs self-assembled quantum dots emitting within a two-dimensional photonic bandgap. A strongly suppressed multiphoton probability is obtained for single quantum dots in bulk GaAs and those emitting into the photonic bandgap. In the latter case, photoluminescence saturation spectroscopy is employed to measure a ~17 times enhancement of the average photon extraction efficiency, when compared to quantum dots in bulk GaAs. For quantum dots in the photonic crystal we measure directly an external quantum efficiency up to 26%, much higher than for quantum dots on the same sample without a tailored photonic environment. The results show that highly efficient quantum dot single photon sources can be realized, without the need for complex nanopositioning techniques.</summary>

    <title>Electronic structures of free-standing nanowires made from indirect bandgap semiconductor gallium phosphide</title>
    <updated>2016-06-08T16:54:38Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1606.02636v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1606.02636v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We present a theoretical study of the electronic structures of freestanding nanowires made from gallium phosphide (GaP)--a III-V semiconductor with an indirect bulk bandgap. We consider [001]-oriented GaP nanowires with square and rectangular cross sections, and [111]-oriented GaP nanowires with hexagonal cross sections. Based on tight binding models, both the band structures and wave functions of the nanowires are calculated. For the [001]-oriented GaP nanowires, the bands show anti-crossing structures, while the bands of the [111]-oriented nanowires display crossing structures. Two minima are observed in the conduction bands, while the maximum of the valence bands is always at the $Î“$-point. Using double group theory, we analyze the symmetry properties of the lowest conduction band states and highest valence band states of GaP nanowires with different sizes and directions. The band state wave functions of the lowest conduction bands and the highest valence bands of the nanowires are evaluated by spatial probability distributions. For practical use, we fit the confinement energies of the electrons and holes in the nanowires to obtain an empirical formula.</summary>
