    <title>A critique of recent semi-classical spin-half quantum plasma theories</title>
    <updated>2014-09-01T16:50:07Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1407.6865v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1407.6865v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>Certain recent semi-classical theories of spin-half quantum plasmas are examined with regard to their internal consistency, physical applicability and relevance to fusion, astrophysical and condensed matter plasmas. It is shown that the derivations and some of the results obtained in these theories are internally inconsistent and contradict well-established principles of quantum and statistical mechanics, especially in their treatment of fermions and spin. Claims of large semi-classical effects of spin magnetic moments that could dominate the plasma dynamics are found to be invalid both for single-particles and collectively. Larmor moments dominate at high temperature while spin moments cancel due to Pauli blocking at low temperatures. Explicit numerical estimates from a variety of plasmas are provided to demonstrate that spin effects are indeed much smaller than many neglected classical effects. The analysis presented suggests that the aforementioned `Spin Quantum Hydrodynamic' theories are not relevant to conventional laboratory or astrophysical plasmas.</summary>

    <title>Spin quantum plasmas - new aspects of collective dynamics</title>
    <updated>2008-04-02T11:40:21Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/0804.0334v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/0804.0334v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  Quantum plasmas is a rapidly expanding field of research, with applications ranging from nanoelectronics, nanoscale devices and ultracold plasmas, to inertial confinement fusion and astrophysics. Here we give a short systematic overview of quantum plasmas. In particular, we analyze the collective effects due to spin using fluid models. The introduction of an intrinsic magnetization due to the plasma electron (or positron) spin properties in the magnetohydrodynamic limit is discussed. Finally, a discussion of the theory and examples of applications is given.</summary>
