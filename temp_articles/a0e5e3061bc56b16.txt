    <title>The role of spin and angular momentum in strong-field ionization</title>
    <updated>2018-02-06T16:10:53Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.03935v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1711.03935v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>The spin polarization of electrons from multiphoton ionization of Xe by 395 nm circularly polarized laser pulses at $6\cdot10^{13}$ W/cm$^2$ has been measured. At this photon energy of 3.14 eV the above threshold ionization peaks connected to Xe$^+$ ions in the ground state ($J=3/2$, ionization potential $I_p=12.1$ eV) and the first exicted state ($J=1/2$, $I_p=13.4$ eV) are clearly separated in the electron energy distribution. These two combs of ATI peaks show opposite spin polarizations. The magnitude of the spin polarization is a factor of two higher for the $J=1/2$ than for the $J=3/2$ final ionic state. In turn the data show that the ionization probability is strongly dependent on the sign of the magnetic quantum number.</summary>

    <title>Double ionization of a three-electron atom: Spin correlation effects</title>
    <updated>2019-12-05T20:46:42Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.08357v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1905.08357v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We study the effects of spin degrees of freedom and wave function symmetries on double ionization in three-electron systems. Each electron is assigned one spatial degree of freedom. The resulting three-dimensional Schrödinger equation is integrated numerically using grid-based Fourier transforms. We reveal three-electron effects on the double ionization yield by comparing signals for different ionization channels. We explain our findings by the existence of fundamental differences between three-electronic and truly two-electronic spin-resolved ionization schemes. We find, for instance, that double ionization from a three-electron system is dominated by electrons that have the opposite spin.</summary>

    <title>Spin-resolved electron-impact ionization of lithium</title>
    <updated>1999-06-21T05:36:42Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/physics/9906033v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/physics/9906033v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  Electron-impact ionization of lithium is studied using the convergent close-coupling (CCC) method at 25.4 and 54.4 eV. Particular attention is paid to the spin-dependence of the ionization cross sections. Convergence is found to be more rapid for the spin asymmetries, which are in good agreement with experiment, than for the underlying cross sections. Comparison with the recent measured and DS3C-calculated data of Streun et al (1999) is most intriguing. Excellent agreement is found with the measured and calculated spin asymmetries, yet the discrepancy between the CCC and DS3C cross sections is very large.</summary>
