    <title>Pion and muon production in electron-positron photon plasma</title>
    <updated>2008-05-28T15:48:21Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/0803.1588v4" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/0803.1588v4" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  We study production and equilibration of pions and muons in relativistic electron-positron-photon plasma at a temperature $T\ll m_μ, m_π$. We argue that the observation of pions and muons can be a diagnostic tool in the study of the initial properties of such a plasma formed by means of strong laser fields. Conversely, properties of muons and pions in thermal environment become accessible to precise experimental study.</summary>

    <title>Electron-muon correlation as a new probe to strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma</title>
    <updated>2009-07-03T10:53:06Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/0907.0588v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/0907.0588v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  As a new and clean probe to the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP), we propose an azimuthal correlation of an electron and a muon which originate from the semileptonic decay of charm and bottom quarks. By solving the Langevin equation for the heavy quarks under the hydrodynamic evolution of the hot plasma, we show that substantial quenching of the away-side peak in the electron-muon correlation can be seen if the sQGP drag force acting on heavy quarks is large enough as suggested from the gauge/gravity correspondence. The effect could be detected in high-energy heavy-ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and the Large Hadron Collider.</summary>

    <title>A muon source based on plasma accelerators</title>
    <updated>2017-11-18T08:13:14Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.06022v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1711.06022v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>The conceptual design of a compact source of GeV-class muons is presented, based on a plasma based electron-gamma collider. Evaluations of muon flux, spectra and brilliance are presented, carried out with ad-hoc montecarlo simulations of the electron-gamma collisions. These are analyzed in the context of a large spread of the invariant mass in the e-gamma interaction, due to the typical characteristics of plasma self-injected GeV electron beams, carrying large bunch charges with huge energy spread. The availability of a compact point-like muon source, triggerable at nsec level, may open a completely new scenario in the muon radiography application field.</summary>
