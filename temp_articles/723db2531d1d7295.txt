    <title>Tachyon Dynamics - for Neutrinos?</title>
    <updated>2017-11-16T16:16:58Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.09904v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1710.09904v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>Following earlier studies that provided a consistent theory of kinematics for tachyons (faster-than-light particles) we here embark on a study of tachyon dynamics, both in classical physics and in the quantum theory. Examining a general scattering process we come to recognize that the labels given to "in" and "out" states are not Lorentz invariant for tachyons; and this lets us find a sensible interpretation of negative energy states. For statistical mechanics, as well as for scattering problems, we study what should be the proper expression for density of states for tachyons. We review the previous work on quantization of a Dirac field for tachyons and go on to expand earlier considerations of neutrinos as tachyons in the context of cosmology. We stumble into the realization that tachyon neutrinos would contribute to gravitation with the opposite sign compared to tachyon antineutrinos. This leads to the gobsmacking prediction that the Cosmic Neutrino Background, if they are indeed tachyons, might explain both phenomena of Dark Matter and Dark Energy. This theoretical study also makes contact with the anticipated results from the experiments KATRIN and PTOLEMY, which focus on beta decay and neutrino absorption by Tritium.</summary>

    <title>Inconsistence of super-luminal Cern-Opera neutrino speed with observed SN1987A burst and neutrino mixing for any imaginary neutrino mass</title>
    <updated>2012-03-05T19:27:45Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1109.5368v6" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1109.5368v6" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We tried to fit in any way the recent Opera-Cern claims of a neutrino super-luminal speed with observed Supernova SN1987A neutrino burst and all (or most) neutrino flavor oscillation. We considered three main frame-works: (1) A tachyon imaginary neutrino mass, whose timing is nevertheless in conflict with observed IMB-Kamiokande SN1987A burst by thousands of billion times longer. (2) An ad hoc anti-tachyon model whose timing shrinkage may accommodate SN1987A burst but greatly disagree with energy independent Cern-Opera super-luminal speed. (3) A split neutrino flavor speed (among a common real mass relativistic neutrino electron component and a super-luminal neutrino Î¼) in an ad hoc frozen speed scenario that is leading to the prompt neutrino de-coherence and the rapid flavor mixing (between electron and muon ones) that are in conflict with most oscillation records. Therefore we concluded that an error must be hidden in Opera-Cern time calibration (as indeed recent rumors seem to confirm). We are also reminding the relevance of the guaranteed minimal atmospheric neutrino mass whose detection may be achieved by a milliseconds graviton-neutrino split time delay among gravity burst and neutronization neutrino peak in any future SN explosion in Andromeda recordable in Megaton neutrino detector.</summary>

    <title>Tritium Decay and the Hypothesis of Tachyonic Neutrinos</title>
    <updated>1998-10-14T12:26:02Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/9810355v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-ph/9810355v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  Numerous recent measurements indicate an excess of counts near the endpoint of the electron energy spectrum in tritium decay. We show that this effect is expected if the neutrino is a tachyon. Results of calculations, based on a unitary (causal) theory of tachyons, are presented. The hypothesis of tachyonic neutrinos also offers a natural explanation of the V-A structure of the weak leptonic current in neutrino interactions.</summary>
