    <title>Zoom in muon survival probability with sterile neutrino for CP and T-violation</title>
    <updated>2022-08-20T14:40:08Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2208.09696v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/2208.09696v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We present the approximated analytic expressions for the muon survival probability in a $3+1$ mixing scenario in the presence of matter effect using the S-matrix formalism. We find that all the individual terms contributing to the muon survival probability can significantly reduce to just three contributions. The leading order contribution comes from the three flavor muon survival probability followed by the two sub-leading contributions arising from active-sterile mixing. Furthermore, to more simplify the results we adopt the well known series expansion relations about mass-hierarchy parameter $α= Δm^2_{21} / Δm^2_{31}$ and the mixing angle $\sin θ_{13}$ in the vanishing limit of $α^2$. We discuss the relevance of muon survival probability to probe the CP and T-violation studies coming from the new physics. We also compare the analytic relation between vacuum and matter contributions to the muon survival probability at the leading order. Finally, we comment on the probability behavior at the various long baselines relevant to understand the atmospheric-neutrino sector and to resolve the existing mass-hierarchy problem.</summary>

    <title>Quasiclassical approach to synergic synchrotron-Cherenkov radiation in polarized vacuum</title>
    <updated>2020-04-24T22:10:12Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.12007v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/2004.12007v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>The photon emission by an ultrarelativistic charged particle in extremely strong magnetic field is analyzed, with vacuum polarization and photon recoil taken into account. The vacuum polarization is treated phenomenologically via refractive index. The photon emission occurs in the synergic (cooperative) synchrotron-Cherenkov process [J. Schwinger, W. Tsai and T. Erber, Annals of Physics, 96 303 (1976)] which is similar to the synchrotron emission rather than to the Cherenkov one. For electrons, the effect of the vacuum polarization on the emission spectrum is not evident even beyond the probable onset of non-perturbative quantum electrodynamics (QED). However, the effect of the vacuum polarization on the emission spectrum can be observable for muons already at $γB / B_S \approx 30$, with $γ$ the muon Lorentz factor, $B$ the magnetic field strength and $B_S$ the critical QED field. Nevertheless, vacuum polarization leads to only 10% enhancement of the maximum of the radiation spectrum.</summary>

    <title>An Update of the HLS Estimate of the Muon g-2</title>
    <updated>2013-05-20T15:25:37Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1210.7184v3" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1210.7184v3" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>A global fit of parameters allows us to pin down the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) effective Lagrangian, which we apply for the prediction of the leading hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the muon g-2. The latter is dominated by the annihilation channel e+e- -&gt; pi+pi-, for which data are available by scan (CMD-2 and SND) and ISR (KLOE-2008, KLOE-2010 and BaBar) experiments. It is well known that the different data sets are not in satisfactory agreement. In fact it is possible to fix the model parameters without using the pi+pi- data, by using instead the dipion spectra measured in the tau decays together with experimental spectra for the pi0 gamma, eta gamma, pi+pi-pi0, K+K-, K0bar K0 final states supplemented by specific meson decay properties. Among these, the accepted decay width for rho0 -&gt; e+e- and the partial widths and phase information for the omega/phi -&gt; pi+pi- transitions, are considered. It is then shown that, relying on this global data set, the HLS model, appropriately broken, allows to predict accurately the pion form factor up to 1.05 GeV. It is shown that the data samples provided by CMD-2, SND and KLOE-2010 behave consistently with each other and with the other considered data. Consistency problems with the KLOE-2008 and BaBar data samples are substantiated. "All data" global fits are investigated by applying reweighting the conflicting data sets. Constraining to our best fit, the broken HLS model yields a_mu(th) = (11659169.55 [+1.26 -0.59]_phi +[+0.00 -2.00]_tau +/- 5.21_(th))~10**-10 associated with a very good global fit probability. Correspondingly, we find that Delta a_mu=a_mu (exp)- a_mu (th) exhibits a significance ranging between 4.7 and 4.9 sigma.</summary>
