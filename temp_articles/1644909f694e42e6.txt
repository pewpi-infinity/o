    <title>Anti-screening ferromagnetic superconductivity</title>
    <updated>2013-12-23T10:31:50Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1311.4519v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1311.4519v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We consider the Ginzburg-Landau mean field theory of ferromagnetic superconductors with complex order parameter $ψ_i, i=1,2,3$. Below the critical temperature superconductivity is generated by means of a tachyonic term in the free energy $-α|ψ_i|^2$ $(α&gt;0)$, stabilized by a $ψ^4$-term. However, we show that when $α$ becomes negative superconductivity can also be generated spontaneously by the magnetic field through the Zeeman like coupling between the field and the spin of the order parameter, in analogy with what happens in W-meson condensation. We also show that this mechanism leads to a vortex lattice which has anti-screening currents, enhancing the magnetic field instead of counteracting it. The lattice of vortices is a collective phenomenon where the individual elements do not exist in isolated form. If the parameter $α$ vanishes it is possible to obtain an explicit form of the vortex lattice in terms of Weierstrass' p-function.</summary>

    <title>Covariant Fermionic Fields of Space-like Particles</title>
    <updated>2020-11-20T14:52:10Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.01183v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/2012.01183v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We develop covariant fermionic fields of space-like particles. As an application of the formalism we discuss the example of superluminous tachyons with imaginary rest mass and spin 1/2 forming fermionic ensembles that is relativistically covariant. We use the tachyonic single particle Hilbert space to build the quantum stochastic fields as this notion is more consensual, instability in vacuum quantum field than faster than light particles, in field theory with applications to superconductivity via tachyonic condensation. We also establish the localizability and as a consequence the causality of tachyons (in terms of anticommutators) using the systems of imprimitivity framework and derive the Dirac equation for the space-like particle that has an additional helicity term.</summary>

    <title>A Constituent Quark Anti-Quark Effective Lagrangian Based on the Dual Superconducting Model of Long Distance QCD</title>
    <updated>1994-03-09T21:01:31Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/9403258v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-ph/9403258v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  We review the assumptions leading to the description of long distance QCD by a Lagrangian density expressed in terms of dual potentials. We find the color field distribution surrounding a quark anti-quark pair to first order in their velocities. Using these distributions we eliminate the dual potentials from the Lagrangian density and obtain an effective interaction Lagrangian $L_I ( \vec x_1 \, , \vec x_2 \, ; \vec v_1 \, , \vec v_2 )$ depending only upon the quark and anti-quark coordinates and velocities, valid to second order in their velocities. We propose $L_I$ as the Lagrangian describing the long distance interaction between constituent quarks. Elsewhere we have determined the two free parameters in $L_I$, $α_s$ and the string tension $σ$, by fitting the 17 known levels of $b \bar b$ and $c \bar c$ systems. Here we use $L_I$ at the classical level to calculate the leading Regge trajectory. We obtain a trajectory which becomes linear at large $M^2$ with a slope $α' \simeq .74 \, \hbox{GeV}^{-1}$, and for small $M^2$ the trajectory bends so that there are no tachyons. For a constituent quark mass between 100 and 150 MeV this trajectory passes through the two known Regge recurrences of the $π$ meson. In this paper, for simplicity of presentation, we have treated the quarks as spin-zero particles.</summary>
