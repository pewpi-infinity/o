    <title>Nitrogen Plasma Passivated Niobium Resonators for Superconducting Quantum Circuits</title>
    <updated>2022-02-18T13:41:51Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.01776v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/2201.01776v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>Microwave loss in niobium metallic structures used for superconducting quantum circuits is limited by a native surface oxide layer formed over a timescale of minutes when exposed to an ambient environment. In this work, we show that nitrogen plasma treatment forms a niobium nitride layer at the metal-air interface which prevents such oxidation. X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy confirms the doping of nitrogen more than 5 nm into the surface and a suppressed oxygen presence. This passivation remains stable after aging for 15 days in an ambient environment. Cryogenic microwave characterization shows an average filling factor adjusted two-level-system loss tangent $\rm{Fδ_{TLS}}$ of $(2.9\pm0.5)\cdot10^{-7}$ for resonators with 3 $\rmμ$m center strip and $(1.0\pm0.3)\cdot10^{-7}$ for 20 $\rmμ$m center strip, exceeding the performance of unpassivated samples by a factor of four.</summary>

    <title>Effect of microwave irradiation on parametric resonance in intrinsic Josephson junctions</title>
    <updated>2012-08-22T10:23:14Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1208.4467v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1208.4467v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>The effect of microwave irradiation on the phase dynamics of intrinsic Josephson junctions in high temperature superconductors is investigated. We demonstrate the influence of microwave's amplitude variation on the current-voltage characteristics and on the time dependence (temporal oscillations) of the electric charge in the superconducting layers. A remarkable changing of the longitudinal plasma wavelength at parametric resonance is shown. We demonstrate an effect of the microwave radiation on the width of the parametric resonance region.</summary>

    <title>Plasma resonance and remaining Josephson coupling in the ``decoupled vortex liquid phase'' in layered superconductors</title>
    <updated>1996-09-05T01:17:25Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/cond-mat/9605067v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/cond-mat/9605067v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  We relate the frequency of the plasma resonance in layered superconductors with the frequency dependent superconducting response along the c-direction. We demonstrate that the sharp resonance can persist even when the global superconducting coherence in this direction is absent provided the resonance frequency is larger than the typical frequency of interlayer phase slips. In this situation the plasma frequency is determined by the average Josephson energy, which can be calculated using the high temperature expansion. We also find the temperature dependence of the average Josephson energy from the Monte Carlo simulations of the uniformly frustrated XY model and determine the applicability region of the high temperature expansion. The field and temperature scaling of the plasma frequency suggested by the high temperature expansion is consistent with experiment.</summary>
