    <title>Quantum valence bond ice theory for proton-driven quantum spin-dipole liquids</title>
    <updated>2020-09-18T06:27:23Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1903.03567v5" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1903.03567v5" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We present a theory of a hybrid quantum liquid state, $\textit{quantum spin-dipole liquid}$ (QSDL), in a hydrogen-bonded electron system, by combining a quantum proton ice and Anderson's resonating valence bond spin liquid theory, motivated by the recent experimental discovery of a quantum spin liquid with proton fluctuations in $Îº$-H$_3$(Cat-EDT-TTF)$_2$ (a.k.a. H-Cat). In our theory, an electron spin liquid and a proton dipole liquid are realized simultaneously in the ground state called $\textit{quantum valence bond ice}$. In this state, neither of them can be established independently of the other. Analytical and numerical calculations reveal that this state has a large entanglement entropy between spins and dipoles, which is far beyond the (crude) Born-Oppenheimer approximation. We also examine the stability of QSDL with respect to perturbations and discuss implications for experiments in H-Cat and its deuterated analog D-Cat.</summary>

    <title>A critical theory of quantum entanglement for the Hydrogen molecule</title>
    <updated>2007-04-11T14:17:56Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/0704.0520v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/0704.0520v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  In this paper we investigate some entanglement properties for the Hydrogen molecule considered as a two interacting spin 1/2 (qubit) model. The entanglement related to the $H_{2}$ molecule is evaluated both using the von Neumann entropy and the Concurrence and it is compared with the corresponding quantities for the two interacting spin system. Many aspects of these functions are examinated employing in part analytical and, essentially, numerical techniques. We have compared analogous results obtained by Huang and Kais a few years ago. In this respect, some possible controversial situations are presented and discussed.</summary>

    <title>Direct calculation of the Entanglement Spectrum in Quantum Monte Carlo with application to \textit{ab initio} Hamiltonians</title>
    <updated>2014-02-03T21:00:03Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1402.0503v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1402.0503v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>Several algorithms have been proposed to calculate the spatial entanglement spectrum from high order Renyi entropies. In this work we present an alternative approach for computing the entanglement spectrum with quantum Monte Carlo for both continuum and lattice Hamiltonians. This method provides direct access to the matrix elements of the spatially reduced density matrix and we determine an estimator that can be used in variational Monte Carlo as well as other Monte Carlo methods. The algorithm is based on using a generalization of the Swap operator, which can be extended to calculate a general class of density matrices that can include combinations of spin, space, particle and even momentum coordinates. We demonstrate the method by applying it to the Hydrogen and Nitrogen molecules and describe for the first time how the spatial entanglement spectrum encodes a covalent bond that includes all the many body correlations.</summary>
