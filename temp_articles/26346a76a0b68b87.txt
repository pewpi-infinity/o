    <title>Dominant next-to-leading order QCD corrections to Higgs plus three jet production in vector-boson fusion</title>
    <updated>2008-04-24T08:25:29Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/0710.5621v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/0710.5621v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  We present the calculation of the dominant next to leading order QCD corrections to Higgs boson production in association with three jets via vector boson fusion in the form of a NLO parton-level Monte Carlo program. QCD corrections to integrated cross sections are modest, while the shapes of some kinematical distributions change appreciably at NLO. Scale uncertainties are shown to be reduced at NLO for the total cross section and for distributions. We consider a central jet veto at the LHC and analyze the veto probability for typical vector boson fusion cuts. Scale uncertainties of the veto probability are sufficiently small at NLO for precise Higgs coupling measurements at the LHC.</summary>

    <title>Intermediate-Mass Higgs Searches in Weak Boson Fusion</title>
    <updated>1999-08-17T16:00:38Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/9908378v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-ph/9908378v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  Weak boson fusion is a copious source of intermediate mass Higgs bosons at the LHC. The additional very energetic forward jets in these events are powerful background suppression tools. I analyze the decays H -&gt; gamma gamma and H -&gt; W^(*)W^(*) -&gt; e mu pTmiss, with the latter a potential discovery channel, and the decay H -&gt; tau tau -&gt; lep had pTmiss as a method for achieving the first direct measurement of a Higgs-fermion coupling.
  I perform parton level analyses of the signal and dominant backgrounds for each decay mode, and demonstrate kinematic cuts and other important tools neccessary to achieve an S/B &gt; 1/1 rate in all cases. I also perform cross section calculations with additional gluon emission which provide an estimate of a minijet veto probability.
  I show that a 5 sigma H -&gt; gamma gamma observation can be made for 110 GeV &lt; M_H &lt; 150 GeV with modest luminosity, order 40-50 fb^-1, overlapping the region explored by the CERN LEP and Fermilab Tevatron. For 130 GeV &lt; M_H &lt; 200 GeV, I show that H -&gt; WW can achieve a 5 sigma observation with S/B much greater than 1/1 with extremely low luminosity, about 2-10 fb^-1 over almost the entire range. This is the most promising search channel in the 130-200 GeV mass range. It overlaps the H -&gt; gamma gamma region and nicely complements the H -&gt; WW measurement that can be made with very low luminosity in inclusive gg -&gt; H production. I further show that a Higgs-fermion coupling can be directly measured via the H -&gt; tau tau decay with only about 60 fb^-1.</summary>

    <title>Higgs production in bottom quark fusion: Matching the 4- and 5-flavour schemes to third order in the strong coupling</title>
    <updated>2020-04-09T18:00:13Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.04752v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/2004.04752v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We present analytic results for the partonic cross sections for the production of a Higgs boson via the fusion of two bottom quarks at N$^3$LO in QCD perturbation theory in the five-flavour scheme. We combine this perturbative result with NLO accurate predictions in the four-flavour scheme that include the full bottom quark mass dependence by appropriately removing any double-counting stemming from contributions included in both predictions. We thereby obtain state-of-the-art predictions for the inclusive production probability of a Higgs boson via bottom quark fusion at hadron colliders.</summary>
