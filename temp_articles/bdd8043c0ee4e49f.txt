    <title>Processing slightly resolved ro-vibrational spectra during chemical vapor deposition of carbon materials: machine learning approach for plasma thermometry</title>
    <updated>2020-11-25T11:13:53Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.12647v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/2011.12647v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>A fast optical spectroscopic method for determination rotational ($T_{rot}$) and vibrational ($T_{vib}$) temperatures in two-temperature Boltzmann distribution of the excited state by using machine learning approach is presented. The method is applied to estimate molecular gas temperatures in a direct current glow discharge in hydrogen-methane gas mixture during plasma-enhanced chemical vapor deposition of carbon film materials. Slightly resolved ro-vibrational optical emission spectrum of the $C_2$ ($ν'=0 \to ν''=0$) Swan band system was used for local temperature measurements in plasma ball. Random Forest algorithm of machine learning was explored for determination of temperature distribution maps. In addition to the $T_{rot}$ , $T_{vib}$ maps, distribution maps and their gradients for electron temperature ($T_e$) and for the emission intensity of the spectral line 516,5$nm$ corresponding to $C_2$ species is presented and is discussed in detail.</summary>
