    <title>The hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the muon g-2 in the quark-resonance model</title>
    <updated>1994-08-04T16:15:39Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/9408231v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-ph/9408231v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  The hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon is parametrized by using the quark-resonance model formulated in \cite{QR}. In this context a recent prediction obtained within the ENJL model \cite{RAF} can be affected by two additional contributions: the next to leading corrections in the inverse cutoff expansion and the gluonic corrections. Motivated by the necessity of reaching a highly accurate theoretical prediction of the hadronic contribution to the muon g-2, we study in detail both the effects.</summary>

    <title>Application of chiral resonance Lagrangian theories to the muon g-2</title>
    <updated>2013-12-13T22:54:17Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1312.3978v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1312.3978v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We think that phenomenological resonance Lagrangian models, constrained by global fits from low energy hadron reaction data, can help to improve muon g-2 predictions. The main issue are those contributions which cannot be calculated by perturbative means: the hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) effects and the hadronic light--by--light (HLbL) scattering contribution. I review recent progress in the evaluation of the HVP contribution within the broken Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) framework, worked out in collaboration with M. Benayoun, P. David and L. DelBuono. Our HLS driven estimate reads a_mu (LO had)=(688.60+\-4.24) 10^{-10} and we find a_mu (theory)= (11 659 177.65 +/- 5.76) 10^{-10}.</summary>

    <title>Subleading hadronic vacuum polarization contributions to muon $g-2$: $μ^+μ^- \rightarrow γ^* \rightarrow π^0 γ^*$</title>
    <updated>2018-01-15T06:16:38Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.07156v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1711.07156v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We consider the subleading contributions of the hadronic vacuum polarization, involving the $π^0γ^*γ^*$ transition form factor, to the muon anomalous magnetic moment $g-2$. Various models for the form factor, based on hadronic ansatzes and holographic principles, are considered: They are the Wess-Zumino-Witten, vector meson dominance, lowest meson dominance (one and two vector resonances) models, and AdS/QCD. The model parameters are determined by fitting the experimental data for the $e^+ e^- \rightarrow π^0 γ$ total cross section. We report numerical results of the corrections to the muon $g-2$ that the resulting values of two vector resonances model are of order-of-magntitude smaller than the one obtained from the dispersion relation.</summary>
