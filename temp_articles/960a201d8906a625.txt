    <title>Double ionization of a three-electron atom: Spin correlation effects</title>
    <updated>2019-12-05T20:46:42Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.08357v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1905.08357v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We study the effects of spin degrees of freedom and wave function symmetries on double ionization in three-electron systems. Each electron is assigned one spatial degree of freedom. The resulting three-dimensional Schrödinger equation is integrated numerically using grid-based Fourier transforms. We reveal three-electron effects on the double ionization yield by comparing signals for different ionization channels. We explain our findings by the existence of fundamental differences between three-electronic and truly two-electronic spin-resolved ionization schemes. We find, for instance, that double ionization from a three-electron system is dominated by electrons that have the opposite spin.</summary>

    <title>Spin polarized electrons produced by strong field ionization</title>
    <updated>2013-03-11T17:08:09Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1212.2895v3" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1212.2895v3" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We show that ionization of noble gas atoms by strong infrared circularly polarized laser field under standard exerimental conditions can yield electrons with up to 100% spin polarization in energy resolved measurements. Spin polarization arises due to the interplay of the electron-core entanglement and the sensitivity of ionization in circularly polarized fields to the sense of electron rotation in the initial state.</summary>

    <id>http://arxiv.org/abs/1702.02778v1</id>
    <title>Electron spin polarization in strong-field ionization of Xenon atoms</title>
    <updated>2017-02-09T10:33:55Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1702.02778v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1702.02778v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>As a fundamental property of the electron, the spin plays a decisive role in the electronic structure of matter from solids to molecules and atoms, e.g. causing magnetism. Yet, despite its importance, the spin dynamics of electrons released during the interaction of atoms with strong ultrashort laser pulses has remained unexplored. Here we report on the experimental detection of electron spin polarization by strong-field ionization of Xenon atoms and support our results by theoretical analysis. We found up to 30% spin polarization changing its sign with electron energy. This work opens the new dimension of spin to strong-field physics. It paves the way to production of subfemtosecond spin polarized electron pulses with applications ranging from probing magnetic properties of matter at ultrafast time scales to testing chiral molecular systems with subfemtosecond temporal and sub-Ångström spatial resolution.</summary>
