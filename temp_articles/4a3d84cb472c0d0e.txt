    <title>Trap induced broadening in a potential hydrogen lattice clock</title>
    <updated>2023-09-27T15:09:14Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.03753v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/2309.03753v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We consider the potential use of optical traps for precision measurements in atomic hydrogen (H). Using an implicit summation method, we calculate the atomic polarisability, the rates of elastic/inelastic scattering and the ionisation rate in the wavelength range 395 to 1000 nm. We extend previous work to predict three new magic wavelengths for the 1S--2S transition. At the magic wavelengths, the 1S--2S transition is unavoidably and significantly broadened due to trap-induced ionisation associated with the high intensity required to trap the 1S state. However, we also find that this effect is partially mitigated by the low mass of H, which increases the trap frequency, enabling Lamb-Dicke confinement in shallow lattices. We find that a H optical lattice clock, free from the motional systematics which dominate in beam experiments, could operate with an intrinsic linewidth of O(1 kHz). Trap-induced losses are shown not to limit measurements of other transitions.</summary>

    <title>Frequency ratio of an $^{115}$In$^+$ ion clock and a $^{87}$Sr optical lattice clock</title>
    <updated>2020-10-30T04:11:06Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2008.06259v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/2008.06259v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We report on the first frequency ratio measurement of an $^{115}$In+ single ion clock and a $^{87}$Sr optical lattice clock. A hydrogen maser serves as a reference oscillator to measure the ratio by independent optical combs. Over more than 90 000 seconds of measurement time, the frequency ratio $f_{\rm{In^+}}/f_{\rm{Sr}}$ is determined to be 2.952 748 749 874 863 4(21) with relative uncertainty of $7.0 \times 10^{-16}$. The measurement creates a new connection in the network of frequency ratios of optical clocks.</summary>
