    <title>Kondo effects in small bandgap carbon nanotube quantum dots</title>
    <updated>2020-09-23T11:00:39Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.11052v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/2009.11052v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We study magnetoconductance of the small bandgap carbon nanotube quantum dots in the presence of spin-orbit coupling in the strong correlations regime. The finite-U mean field slave boson approach is used to study many-body effects. Different degeneracies are restored in magnetic field and Kondo effects of different symmetries arise including SU(3) effects of different types. Full spin-orbital degeneracy might be recovered for zero field and correspondingly SU(4) Kondo effect sets in. We point out on the possibility of the occurrence of electron-hole Kondo effects in slanting magnetic fields, which we predict will occur in the available magnetic fields for orientation of fields close to perpendicular. When the field approaches transverse orientation a crossover from SU(2) or SU(3) symmetry into SU(4) is observed.</summary>

    <title>Gap solitons in superfluid boson-fermion mixtures</title>
    <updated>2007-12-26T13:33:23Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/0712.3947v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/0712.3947v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  Using coupled equations for the bosonic and fermionic order parameters, we construct families of gap solitons (GSs) in a nearly one-dimensional Bose-Fermi mixture trapped in a periodic optical-lattice (OL) potential, the boson and fermion components being in the states of the BEC and BCS superfluid, respectively. Fundamental GSs are compact states trapped, essentially, in a single cell of the lattice. Full families of such solutions are constructed in the first two bandgaps of the OL-induced spectrum, by means of variational and numerical methods, which are found to be in good agreement. The families include both intra-gap and inter-gap solitons, with the chemical potentials of the boson and fermion components falling in the same or different bandgaps, respectively.Nonfundamental states, extended over several lattice cells, are constructed too. The GSs are stable against strong perturbations.</summary>

    <title>Kondo effect near the Van Hove singularity in biased bilayer graphene</title>
    <updated>2012-06-20T10:58:42Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1206.4455v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1206.4455v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>Magnetic impurity adsorbed on one of the carbon planes of a bilayer graphene is studied. The formation of the many-body SU(2) and SU(4) resonances close to the bandgap is analyzed within the mean field Kotliar-Ruckenstein slave boson approach. Impact of enhanced hybridization and magnetic instability of bilayer doped near the Van Hove singularity on the screening of magnetic moment is discussed.</summary>
