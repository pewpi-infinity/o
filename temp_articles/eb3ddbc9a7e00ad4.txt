    <title>Pseudo-spin of time-like lepton and the solar neutrino problem</title>
    <updated>1999-08-12T22:20:59Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/9908344v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-ph/9908344v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  Based on the dual principle in super-luminous Lorentz transformation this work shows that pseudo-spin of a time-like bradyon appears to space-like observers as iso-spin of a corresponding tachyon. Due to the weak interaction, lepton-tachyon appears as neutrino with hidden imaginary transcendent mass, suppressed by a factor of $œÅ\sim G_F m^2_0$ compared to the rest mass $m_0$ of a corresponding space-like lepton. Assuming a coexistance of tachyon dark matter in the solar system, we show that the solar neutrino deficit might be explained by depolarisation of time-like electrons due to elastic scattering in time-like plasma and the maximal deficit would reach 0.5. Neither day-night nor seasonal effects are expected due to dynamic balance of fluxes with opposite pseudo-spin polarisation.</summary>
