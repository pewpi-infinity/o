    <title>MuLan Measurement of the Positive Muon Lifetime and Determination of the Fermi Constant</title>
    <updated>2013-01-03T17:03:00Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1301.0504v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1301.0504v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>We report results from the MuLan measurement of the positive muon lifetime. The experiment was conducted at the Paul Scherrer Institute using a time-structured surface muon beam and a segmented plastic scintillator array. Two different in-vacuum muon stopping targets were used: a ferromagnetic foil with a large internal magnetic field and a quartz crystal in a moderate external magnetic field. From a total of 1.6 x 10^{12} decays, we obtained the muon lifetime tau_mu = 2196980.3(2.2) ps (1.0 ppm) and Fermi constant G_F = 1.1663787(6) x 10^{-5} GeV^{-2} (0.5 ppm).</summary>

    <id>http://arxiv.org/abs/2108.09182v1</id>
    <title>Mulan: a part-per-million measurement of the muon lifetime and determination of the Fermi constant</title>
    <updated>2021-08-20T13:57:31Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.09182v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/2108.09182v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>The part-per-million measurement of the positive muon lifetime and determination of the Fermi constant by the MuLan experiment at the Paul Scherrer Institute is reviewed. The experiment used an innovative, time-structured, surface muon beam and a near-4pi, finely-segmented, plastic scintillator positron detector. Two in-vacuum muon stopping targets were used: a ferromagnetic foil with a large internal magnetic field, and a quartz crystal in a moderate external magnetic field. The experiment obtained a muon lifetime 2 196 980.3(2.2) ps (1.0 ppm) and a Fermi constant 1.166 378 7(6) 10^-5 GeV^-2 (0.5 ppm). The thirty-fold improvement in the muon lifetime has proven valuable for precision measurements in nuclear muon capture and the commensurate improvement in the Fermi constant has proven valuable for precision tests of the standard model.</summary>

    <title>Design and status of the Mu2e crystal calorimeter</title>
    <updated>2018-02-18T08:11:53Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1802.06346v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/1802.06346v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>The Mu2e experiment at Fermilab searches for the charged-lepton flavour violating (CLFV) conversion of a negative muon into an electron in the field of an aluminum nucleus, with a distinctive signature of a mono-energetic electron of energy slightly below the muon rest mass (104.967 MeV). The Mu2e goal is to improve by four orders of magnitude the search sensitivity with respect to the previous experiments. Any observation of a CLFV signal will be a clear indication of new physics. The Mu2e detector is composed of a tracker, an electro- magnetic calorimeter and an external veto for cosmic rays surrounding the solenoid. The calorimeter plays an important role in providing particle identification capabilities, a fast online trigger filter, a seed for track reconstruction while working in vacuum, in the presence of 1 T axial magnetic field and in an harsh radiation environment. The calorimeter requirements are to provide a large acceptance for 100 MeV electrons and reach at these energies: (a) a time resolution better than 0.5 ns; (b) an energy resolution &lt; 10% and (c) a position resolution of 1 cm. The calorimeter design consists of two disks, each one made of 674 undoped CsI crystals read by two large area arrays of UV-extended SiPMs. We report here the construction and test of the Module-0 prototype. The Module-0 has been exposed to an electron beam in the energy range around 100 MeV at the Beam Test Facility in Frascati. Preliminary results of timing and energy resolution at normal incidence are shown. A discussion of the technical aspects of the calorimeter engineering is also reported in this paper.</summary>
