    <title>Hamiltonian lattice gauge theory: wavefunctions on large lattices</title>
    <updated>1992-11-10T20:21:08Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-lat/9211025v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-lat/9211025v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  We discuss an algorithm for the approximate solution of Schrodinger's equation for lattice gauge theory, using lattice SU(3) as an example. A basis is generated by repeatedly applying an effective Hamiltonian to a ``starting state.'' The resulting basis has a cluster decomposition and long-range correlations. One such basis has about 10^4 states on a 10X10X10 lattice. The Hamiltonian matrix on the basis is sparse, and the elements can be calculated rapidly. The lowest eigenstates of the system are readily calculable.</summary>

    <title>Mesons in Transverse Lattice QCD at Strong Coupling and Large-N</title>
    <updated>2001-11-23T08:06:05Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-lat/0111046v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-lat/0111046v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  Mesons in large-N QCD are analysed in light-front coordinates with a transverse lattice at strong coupling. In this limit, their properties can be expressed as simple renormalisations of the 't Hooft model results. The integral eigenvalue equation for the mesons is derived. Spectrum, lightcone wavefunctions and form factors of various mesons can be numerically calculated from this equation.</summary>

    <title>Large-N QCD at strong transverse lattice gauge coupling</title>
    <updated>2005-11-29T12:25:49Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-lat/0511060v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-lat/0511060v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  We had previously obtained an integral equation for mesons in transverse lattice QCD, in the limit of large number of colours and strong transverse lattice gauge coupling [1]. This equation is a generalisation of the 't Hooft equation [2], by inclusion of the spin degrees of freedom. We analyse this equation to extract spectral properties and light-front wavefunctions of mesons. We also extend the method to study baryon properties in the same limit.</summary>
