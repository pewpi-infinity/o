    <title>Neutrino emission and cooling rates of spin-one color superconductors</title>
    <updated>2006-01-26T17:29:19Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/0510347v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-ph/0510347v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  Neutrino emissivities due to direct Urca processes of several spin-one color-superconducting phases of dense quark matter are calculated. In particular, the role of anisotropies and nodes of the gap functions is analyzed. Results for the specific heat as well as for the cooling rates of the color-spin-locked, planar, polar, and {\it A} phases are presented and consequences for the physics of neutron stars are briefly discussed. Furthermore, it is shown that the {\em A} phase exhibits a helicity order, giving rise to a reflection asymmetry in the neutrino emissivity.</summary>

    <title>Phase space and quark mass effects in neutrino emissions in a color superconductor</title>
    <updated>2006-06-30T01:52:08Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/0605092v2" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-ph/0605092v2" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  We study the phase space for neutrino emissions with massive quarks in direct Urca processes in normal and color superconducting quark matter. We derive in QCD and the NJL model the Fermi momentum reduction resulting from Fermi liquid properties which opens up the phase space for neutrino emissions. The relation between the Fermi momentum and chemical potential is found to be $p_{F}\approxμ(1-κ)$ with $κ$ depending on coupling constants. We find in the weak coupling regime that $κ$ is a monotonously increasing function of the chemical potential. This implies quenched phase space for neutrino emissions at low baryon densities. We calculate neutrino emissivities with massive quarks in a spin-one color superconductor. The quark mass corrections are found to be of the same order as the contributions in the massless case, which will bring sizable effects on the cooling behavior of compact stars.</summary>

    <id>http://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/0502166v3</id>
    <title>Pulsar kicks via spin-1 color superconductivity</title>
    <updated>2005-10-04T09:23:16Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/0502166v3" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-ph/0502166v3" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  We propose a new neutrino propulsion mechanism for neutron stars which can lead to strong velocity kicks, needed to explain the observed bimodal velocity distribution of pulsars. The spatial asymmetry in the neutrino emission is naturally provided by a stellar core containing spin-1 color-superconducting quark matter in the A phase. The neutrino propulsion mechanism switches on when the stellar core temperature drops below the transition temperature of this phase.</summary>
