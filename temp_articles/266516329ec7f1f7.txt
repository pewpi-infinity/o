    <title>Color, Spin and Flavor Diffusion in Quark-Gluon Plasmas</title>
    <updated>1994-01-24T22:49:59Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/9401317v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-ph/9401317v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  In weakly interacting quark-gluon plasmas diffusion of color is found to be much slower than the diffusion of spin and flavor because color is easily exchanged by the gluons in the very singular forward scattering processes.  If the infrared divergence is cut off by a magnetic mass, $m_{mag}\sim α_sT$, the color diffusion is $D_{color}\sim (α_s\ln(1/α_s)T)^{-1}$, a factor $α_s$ smaller than spin and flavor diffusion. A similar effect is expected in electroweak plasmas above $M_W$ due to $W^\pm$ exchanges. The color conductivity in quark-gluon plasmas and the electrical conductivity in electroweak plasmas are correspondingly small in relativistic heavy ion collisions and the very early universe.</summary>

    <title>Efficient generation and amplification of intense vortex and vector laser pulses via strongly coupled stimulated Brillouin scattering in plasmas</title>
    <updated>2023-11-23T10:32:23Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.13900v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/2311.13900v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>The past decade has seen tremendous progress in the production and utilization of vortex and vector laser pulses. Although both are considered as structured light beams, the vortex lasers have helical phase fronts and phase singularities, while the vector lasers have spatially variable polarization states and polarization singularities. In contrast to the vortex pulses that carry orbital angular momentum (OAM), the vector laser pulses have a complex spin angular momentum (SAM) and OAM coupling. Despite many potential applications enabled by such pulses, the generation of high-power/-intensity vortex and vector beams remains challenging. Here, we demonstrate using theory and three-dimensional simulations that the strongly-coupled stimulated Brillouin scattering (SC-SBS) process in plasmas can be used as a promising amplification technique with up to 65% energy transfer efficiency from the pump beam to the seed beam for both vortex and vector pulses. We also show that SC-SBS is strongly polarization-dependent in plasmas, enabling an all-optical polarization control of the amplified seed beam. Additionally, the interaction of such structured lasers with plasmas leads to various angular momentum couplings and decouplings that produce intense new light structures with controllable OAM and SAM. This scheme paves the way for novel optical devices such as plasma-based amplifiers and light field manipulators.</summary>

    <title>The Prevention of Singularities Inside Black Holes</title>
    <updated>2005-10-13T20:17:50Z</updated>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/abs/gr-qc/0510065v1" rel="alternate" type="text/html"/>
    <link href="https://arxiv.org/pdf/gr-qc/0510065v1" rel="related" type="application/pdf" title="pdf"/>
    <summary>  In this paper the currently held view that the endpoint of gravitational collapse is a singularity is refuted. A quantum mechanical calculation is done showing that spin 0 and spin 1/2 particles inside a black hole's schwarzschild radius aren't confined to an infinitesimal point but form bound state orbits. As with the case of electric collapse, if an electron cannot spiral into a nucleus, then neither does this happen in plasma consisting of many electrons and nuclei. Showing that after undergoing gravitational collapse, like plasma, matter will not contract to an infinitesimal point. That quantum effects prevent singularity formation.</summary>
